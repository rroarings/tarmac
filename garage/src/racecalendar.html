Scare in the Kingdom

Lewis Hamilton was able to clinch victory at the 2022 British Grand Prix at his home crowd. The race started with a crash at the start involving driver Zhou Guanyu. Despite the incident, the race continued, and Hamilton defended from his rivals to take the checkered flag. The win marked Hamilton's fourth victory at the British Grand Prix and his second win of the 2022 season.

Circuit of The Americas

Max Verstappen takes record-equalling victory. Red Bull also claims their first Constructors' title in nine seasons. There were several retirements during the race, including Valtteri Bottas of Alfa Romeo, who spun off, and Carlos Sainz Jr. of Ferrari, who suffered collision damage. George Russell set the fastest lap of the race.

No Pain at Spain

The Red Bulls securing first and second, Verstappen and Perez claim the most points for the team. pole-sitter and championship leader, Charles Leclerc, was forced to retire due to engine issues. Post Drivers' Championship standings switched from a 19-point Leclerc lead to a 6-point advantage for Verstappen.

Austria, not Australia

Sainz retires from engine and throttle issues, while teammate Leclerc is able to stay ahead of Red Bull's Max Verstappen, who's dealing with tire issues. Leclerc maintains the pole position and wins the race by 1.6 seconds.

Bringing the classics back.

One of the most exciting aspects of the 2022 race calendar is the return of several classic tracks, including the Dutch Grand Prix at Zandvoort and the French Grand Prix at Circuit Paul Ricard. These races hold a special place in the hearts of F1 fans, and their return is sure to add to the excitement and nostalgia of the season. At the same time, the calendar also includes new additions like the Jeddah street circuit in Saudi Arabia and the Miami street circuit in the United States, which promise to offer fresh challenges and thrills for drivers and fans alike. Overall, the 2022 F1 race calendar is shaping up to be one of the most exciting and diverse yet, and is sure to provide plenty of unforgettable moments for fans around the world.

A global sport reaching populations across the world.

The F1 race calendar for the 2022 season is a reflection of the sport's global popularity, featuring 22 races across five continents. From the iconic circuits of Europe to the exciting street courses of Asia and the Americas, the calendar offers a diverse and challenging set of races that will push drivers to their limits.
