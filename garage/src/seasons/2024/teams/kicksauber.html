Kick Sauber, in its final year before becoming Audi in 2026, fields Nico Hülkenberg and F2 champion Gabriel Bortoleto in the C45, the slowest car in Bahrain testing (+1.079s vs. 2024). Hülkenberg, returning from Haas, provides stability, but Bortoleto’s inexperience showed in a near-collision with Fernando Alonso in Jeddah and a retirement in China, leaving Sauber last in early constructor predictions. The C45’s updated livery, revealed at Hinwil, aims to boost visibility amid their transitional phase.

Jonathan Wheatley joins as Team Principal in April, tasked with steering Sauber toward Audi’s ambitious future. The C45 struggles with downforce and tyre wear, limiting Hülkenberg to P14 in Australia, though his experience mitigates some setup issues. Bortoleto’s raw pace, evident in F2, requires honing to handle F1’s demands, with the team leaning on Hülkenberg’s feedback to guide development in a critical season.

Sauber’s primary challenge is their uncompetitive C45, which lags behind Alpine and Haas, coupled with Bortoleto’s steep learning curve on complex tracks like Suzuka. Wheatley’s late arrival delays strategic changes, and the team’s focus on 2026 risks 2025 performance. With a grueling 24-race calendar, Sauber aims for occasional points to build morale, hoping Hülkenberg’s consistency and Bortoleto’s growth lay groundwork for Audi’s 2026 debut.
