Max Verstappen’s 2024 season was a masterclass in consistency, securing his fourth consecutive Drivers’ Championship despite Red Bull’s mid-season struggles. He started dominantly, winning seven of the first ten races, including Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and Japan, where his pole-to-flag victories showcased the RB20’s early superiority. His standout performances included a wet-weather masterclass in Brazil, winning from 17th on the grid, and holding off Lando Norris in Imola by just 0.725 seconds, demonstrating his ability to extract maximum performance under pressure. Verstappen’s 362 points and nine Grand Prix wins underscored his relentless pace, even as McLaren and Ferrari closed the gap.

The second half of the season tested Verstappen’s resilience as Red Bull’s car lost its edge to McLaren’s upgrades. He went 14 races without a win after Spain, facing challenges like a poor start in Austria, where a collision with Norris cost him a potential victory, and a penalty-laden Qatar race. Despite these setbacks, Verstappen’s recovery drives, such as gaining 16 places in Brazil, and his ability to capitalize on rivals’ errors ensured he clinched the title in Las Vegas with two races to spare. His 560 laps led—more than twice any other driver—highlighted his dominance, though he admitted the RB20’s unpredictability made wins harder to achieve.

Verstappen’s 2024 campaign solidified his status as F1’s benchmark, but it also exposed Red Bull’s reliance on his brilliance, as teammate Sergio Perez’s struggles left them third in the Constructors’ Championship. His ability to adapt to a less competitive car, combined with strategic brilliance in races like Hungary, where he recovered to fifth despite team strategy issues, earned him widespread praise from peers, with team bosses voting him the top driver of 2024. Verstappen’s season was a testament to his skill and mental fortitude, setting the stage for a fierce 2025 battle with McLaren and Ferrari.
