Zhou Guanyu’s 2024 season with Sauber was a struggle, defined by an uncompetitive car and limited opportunities to shine, though he managed a standout result late in the year. Driving for a Sauber team plagued by technical issues and a lack of pace, Zhou spent much of the season battling at the back of the grid. His qualifying performances were often outshone by teammate Valtteri Bottas, who regularly showed the car’s true potential, but Zhou’s race pace occasionally kept him in contention, as seen in races like Qatar, where he secured an impressive eighth place for his and Sauber’s first points of the season.

The season was frustrating for Zhou, with Sauber’s early pit stop issues in Australia and Japan costing potential points when the car was marginally more competitive. His consistency was a strength, with fewer mistakes than some peers, but the C44’s lack of development left him and Bottas anonymous for much of the year. Zhou’s best moments came in high-pressure situations, like his Qatar performance, where he capitalized on a chaotic race to score four points. However, incidents like being hit by debris in Abu Dhabi while being lapped highlighted the team’s struggles and his misfortune in an uncompetitive machine.

Zhou finished 21st in the Drivers’ Championship with four points, a meager return that reflected Sauber’s dire season rather than his ability. His departure from Sauber, alongside Bottas, marked the end of his three-year stint, with no confirmed seat for 2025. While his Qatar result showed his potential to deliver in the midfield, the lack of standout performances and Sauber’s focus on new drivers like Gabriel Bortoleto for 2025 left Zhou’s F1 future uncertain, though links with Cadillac suggest a possible return in 2026.
