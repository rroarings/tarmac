Daniel Ricciardo’s 2024 season with RB was a challenging return to Formula 1, ending with his departure before the season’s conclusion. He started with a highlight in the Miami sprint, finishing fourth to score his first points of the season, a rare bright spot in a year where RB’s car struggled to compete consistently. Ricciardo’s experience showed in moments like Japan, where teammate Yuki Tsunoda scored a point, but Ricciardo’s race was cut short by a heavy crash with Alex Albon, triggering a red flag. His early-season form suggested he could still deliver, but consistency eluded him.

The mid-season was particularly tough, with Ricciardo failing to progress beyond Q1 in races like Canada, where RB’s upgrades didn’t deliver the expected pace. His main race in Miami was a low point, described as “limp” with no progress from a strong sprint, and his Azerbaijan performance was lackluster, compounded by a late crash with Perez. Red Bull’s decision to replace him with Liam Lawson from the United States Grand Prix onward was driven by Helmut Marko’s assessment that Ricciardo had lost his “killer instinct,” a harsh critique of his fading competitiveness. Ricciardo’s struggles in qualifying and races, like finishing under a second behind Hamilton’s Q1 benchmark in Canada, underscored his difficulties.

Ricciardo ended the season 17th in the Drivers’ Championship with 12 points, level with Alex Albon but far below expectations for a driver of his pedigree. His marketability, especially in the U.S., remained a strength, but his lack of standout results and inability to match Tsunoda’s pace in the same car led to his exit. Ricciardo’s 2024 season suggested he may have raced his last F1 Grand Prix, with his focus shifting to interests outside the sport, leaving fans to wonder if his charismatic presence will return to the paddock.
