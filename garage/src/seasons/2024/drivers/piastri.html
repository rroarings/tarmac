Oscar Piastri’s 2024 season was a breakout sophomore year, securing his maiden Grand Prix win in Hungary and finishing fifth in the Drivers’ Championship with 237 points. His victory, aided by Norris obeying team orders, highlighted his speed and composure, while his second win in Belgium, inherited after Russell’s disqualification, underscored McLaren’s competitive MCL38. Piastri’s eight podiums, including a close second to Hamilton in Belgium (0.647s gap), and consistent points finishes were pivotal in McLaren’s Constructors’ Championship triumph.

Piastri’s season wasn’t flawless, with errors like a poor start in Austria, where he was pincered between Perez and Piastri, forcing a first-lap pit stop and an 11th-place finish. His qualifying pace occasionally lagged behind Norris, as seen in Canada, where he struggled to extract the car’s full potential. McLaren’s strategic missteps, like delaying his Silverstone pit stop, also cost him potential podiums, but his ability to recover, such as finishing fourth in Qatar, showed his growing maturity.

Piastri’s 2024 campaign marked him as a rising star, with his Hungary win and consistent top-five finishes earning a contract extension through 2026. His ability to match Norris’s pace in races, despite fewer poles, and his role in McLaren’s title success highlighted his potential. For 2025, Piastri will aim to close the qualifying gap and capitalize on McLaren’s momentum to challenge for more wins.
