The 2024 Formula 1 race season at your fingertips

1 Bahrain

The 2024 F1 season kicked off in Bahrain with a commanding win by Max Verstappen, as Red Bull locked out the top two spots. Ferrari showed flashes of competitiveness, but Leclerc’s brake issues cost them a stronger result. Mercedes lagged behind, while McLaren and Aston Martin capitalized on opportunities, hinting at an exciting season ahead.

2 Saudi Arabia

Max Verstappen dominated the 2024 Saudi Arabian Grand Prix, securing a commanding victory ahead of Red Bull teammate Sergio Perez and Ferrari’s Charles Leclerc. The race also saw an impressive debut from Oliver Bearman, who scored points in his first-ever F1 race.

3 Australia

The 2024 Australian Grand Prix saw a dramatic shift in the season’s momentum, with Carlos Sainz delivering a dominant win for Ferrari as Verstappen retired due to mechanical issues. Charles Leclerc secured second, while Lando Norris rounded out the podium in a race filled with surprises, including George Russell’s crash.

4 Japan

Red Bull reigned supreme in Japan as Max Verstappen powered to victory, with teammate Sergio Perez securing second for a commanding 1-2 finish. The race featured early chaos, a red flag, and a strong drive from Carlos Sainz to complete the podium.

5 China

Max Verstappen triumphed in an action-packed Chinese Grand Prix, fending off Lando Norris and Sergio Perez to secure victory. The race featured multiple incidents, a Safety Car intervention, and a dramatic restart.

6 Miami

The Miami Grand Prix saw Lando Norris claim his first-ever F1 win in dramatic fashion, outpacing Max Verstappen and Charles Leclerc. The McLaren driver delivered a standout performance in an action-packed race that kept fans on edge.

7 Emilia-Romagna

In a nail-biting Emilia-Romagna Grand Prix, Max Verstappen fended off a relentless late attack from Lando Norris to clinch victory by less than a second, while Charles Leclerc completed the podium in third.

8 Monaco

In a fairytale finish, Charles Leclerc dominated the streets of Monaco to claim his long-awaited home victory, leading Oscar Piastri and Carlos Sainz to the podium after an early chaotic start.

9 Canada

Verstappen displayed perfect racecraft in the unpredictable conditions of the Canadian Grand Prix, emerging victorious after a wet-dry race, with Lando Norris and George Russell finishing behind in a competitive field.

10 Spain

In a nail-biting finish, Max Verstappen just held off Lando Norris to take victory in the Spanish Grand Prix, while Lewis Hamilton earned his first podium of the season in a thrilling race.

11 Austria

In a dramatic turn of events, George Russell snatched an unexpected victory at the Austrian Grand Prix after Max Verstappen and Lando Norris collided while fighting for the lead.

12 Great Britain

In a thrilling wet-dry race at Silverstone, Lewis Hamilton clinched his ninth British Grand Prix victory, narrowly beating Max Verstappen, with Lando Norris finishing third.

13 Hungary

In a dramatic Hungarian Grand Prix, Oscar Piastri took his first F1 win with McLaren dominating the race, while Lando Norris reluctantly handed back the lead. Lewis Hamilton secured third after a collision with Verstappen.

14 Belgium

Lewis Hamilton took the win at Spa after a dramatic race in which his Mercedes teammate, George Russell, was disqualified. Oscar Piastri finished second, while Charles Leclerc completed the podium in third.

15 Netherlands

Lando Norris triumphed at the Dutch Grand Prix, taking the lead from Max Verstappen and denying the home hero a continued streak of wins. Charles Leclerc finished third in a competitive race at Zandvoort.

16 Italy

Leclerc thrilled the Tifosi with a fairytale win at Monza, edging out McLaren's Piastri and Norris, as Ferrari's daring strategy paid off for an emotional home triumph.

17 Azerbaijan

Oscar Piastri secured a dramatic win at the Azerbaijan GP, holding off Charles Leclerc in a tense final race battle, as Carlos Sainz and Sergio Perez collided on the penultimate lap.

18 Singapore

Lando Norris was untouchable in the Singapore Grand Prix, delivering a lights-to-flag victory. Max Verstappen finished second, splitting the McLaren pair, with Oscar Piastri securing third. Norris’ win once again chipping away at Verstappen’s lead in the championship.
