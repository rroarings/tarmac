'24 F1 Season: An Unforgettable Ride

May 21, 2025 by Nick

The 2024 FIA Formula One World Championship was a thrilling saga of speed, drama, and history-making moments. Max Verstappen claimed his fourth straight Drivers’ title, McLaren ended a 26-year wait for the Constructors’ crown, and drivers like Lando Norris and Lewis Hamilton delivered iconic victories. Relive the season’s most unforgettable moments, from Miami’s surprises to Abu Dhabi’s finale.

A Season of Triumphs and Twists

The 2024 Formula 1 season, the 75th in FIA history, spanned a record 24 Grands Prix and six sprints, delivering non-stop action from Bahrain to Abu Dhabi. Max Verstappen dominated early, winning four of the first five races, but McLaren’s resurgence, led by Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri, challenged Red Bull’s grip, culminating in McLaren’s Constructors’ title at the finale. Ferrari, Mercedes, and even underdog teams like Alpine shone in iconic races, while off-track shocks like Lewis Hamilton’s Ferrari move for 2025 kept fans buzzing. Here’s a breakdown of the season’s defining moments, from epic battles to emotional farewells.

Verstappen’s Fourth Title

Max Verstappen started 2024 with a bang, winning four of the first five races, including a dominant Grand Chelem in Bahrain. Despite McLaren’s MCL38 outpacing Red Bull’s RB20 mid-season, Verstappen’s consistency shone, with standout drives like his wet-weather masterclass from P17 to P1 in Sao Paulo. He clinched his fourth consecutive Drivers’ Championship in Las Vegas, joining legends like Schumacher and Hamilton. His ability to score double-digit points in nearly every race, even without the fastest car, cemented his status as a modern great.

McLaren’s Constructors’ Triumph

McLaren ended a 26-year drought to claim the 2024 Constructors’ Championship in Abu Dhabi, their first since 1998. Lando Norris’s fourth win of the season sealed the title, with Oscar Piastri’s support crucial. The team’s turnaround began in Miami, where Norris’s maiden victory marked their rise. Despite Red Bull’s early dominance, McLaren’s upgrades made the MCL38 the grid’s fastest car, outscoring rivals in key races like Singapore and Zandvoort. Their 1-2 in Hungary, though marred by team orders drama, showcased their strength.

Unforgettable Victories That Shaped 2024

The 2024 season saw seven different winners from four teams, a stark contrast to 2023’s Red Bull dominance. Lando Norris broke through with his first win in Miami, capitalizing on a safety car to beat Verstappen. Charles Leclerc ended his “Monaco curse,” becoming the first Monégasque to win his home race since 1950. Lewis Hamilton’s emotional ninth British Grand Prix victory at Silverstone ended a 945-day winless streak, thrilling the home crowd. Carlos Sainz’s stunning Australian Grand Prix win, just 16 days after surgery, and Ferrari’s 1-2 finish marked their resurgence. These victories defined a season of unpredictability.

New Faces and Shocking Changes

Rookies and mid-season swaps added spice to 2024. Oliver Bearman, an 18-year-old Ferrari reserve, debuted in Saudi Arabia, scoring P7 and later points with Haas in Azerbaijan, earning a 2025 Haas seat. Franco Colapinto replaced Logan Sargeant at Williams from the Italian Grand Prix, scoring points in Azerbaijan. Daniel Ricciardo was dropped by RB after Singapore, with Liam Lawson stepping in, signaling Red Bull’s youth focus. Lewis Hamilton’s bombshell announcement to join Ferrari in 2025, revealed before the season, set the stage for a dramatic farewell with Mercedes.

Beyond the Track: 2024’s Biggest Stories

Off-track narratives shaped 2024 as much as the racing. Lewis Hamilton’s Ferrari move for 2025 sent shockwaves, while Adrian Newey’s exit from Red Bull to join Aston Martin raised eyebrows. RB’s team orders controversy in Bahrain sparked tensions between Yuki Tsunoda and Daniel Ricciardo. Alpine’s double podium in Sao Paulo, led by Esteban Ocon and Pierre Gasly, was a surprising turnaround after a dismal start. These moments, alongside regulatory changes like increased power unit allocations, set the stage for a seismic 2025 season with new drivers like Kimi Antonelli at Mercedes

2024: A Season to Remember

From Verstappen’s relentless title charge to McLaren’s triumphant Constructors’ win, 2024 was a Formula 1 season of heart-pounding races and game-changing moments. Iconic victories, rookie heroics, and off-track shocks kept fans glued to the action. Dive deeper into the drivers and teams that made it unforgettable, and gear up for 2025’s new battles with Hamilton at Ferrari and more!
