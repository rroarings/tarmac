From the Trackside

COTA's Thrilling Peaks

May 21, 2025 by Nick

The 2024 Formula 1 United States Grand Prix at Circuit of The Americas delivered heart-pounding action under Austin’s vibrant skies. Ferrari’s Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz Jr. stunned with a 1-2 finish, while Max Verstappen and Lando Norris battled fiercely, only for a late penalty to shake up the podium.

COTA 2024: A Race Packed with Drama

Leclerc and Sainz Steal the Show

Charles Leclerc delivered a masterclass at COTA, starting from P4 but surging to the lead at Turn 1 after Lando Norris and Max Verstappen tangled. Leclerc’s untouchable pace in the Ferrari SF-24 saw him pull away, while teammate Carlos Sainz Jr. used a slick undercut strategy during pit stops to secure P2, overtaking Verstappen. This 1-2 finish, Ferrari’s first in the US since 2006, marked their first US win since Kimi Räikkönen’s 2018 triumph. The red cars shone under Austin’s lights, boosting Ferrari’s Constructors’ title hopes and thrilling fans.

Verstappen Holds Off Norris in Epic Duel

Max Verstappen, starting P2, showcased why he’s a four-time champion with a defensive masterclass against Lando Norris. After losing ground at the start, Verstappen fought back, holding off Norris’s McLaren in a tense late-race battle through COTA’s tricky Turns 13-15. Norris briefly overtook Verstappen on lap 52, but a controversial move off-track at Turn 12 led to a five-second penalty, dropping Norris from P3 to P4. Verstappen’s P3 finish extended his championship lead, proving his skill under pressure.

Norris’s Pole-to-Penalty Heartbreak

Lando Norris started on pole, a career highlight, but his lead vanished at Turn 1 when Verstappen’s aggressive inside move pushed him wide. Norris fought back valiantly, closing the gap to Verstappen in the final laps and briefly taking P3 with a bold overtake at Turn 12. However, a five-second penalty for gaining an advantage off-track demoted him to P4, a gut-punch for McLaren fans. Despite the setback, Norris’s pace showed McLaren’s strength, setting the stage for their Constructors’ title push. Liam Lawson Shines for RB

Liam Lawson, RB’s replacement for Daniel Ricciardo, made waves at COTA, starting from P19 due to a power unit penalty but charging to an impressive P9. His aggressive overtakes through COTA’s high-speed Maggotts-Becketts-inspired section (Turns 3-6) showcased his talent, earning crucial points for RB in their fight with Haas for P6 in the Constructors’ standings. Lawson’s performance solidified his case for a 2025 Red Bull seat alongside Verstappen, marking him as a rising star.

COTA’s Colorful Spectacle

The 2024 USGP wasn’t just about racing—it was a visual feast. McLaren’s chrome livery, inspired by Google Chrome, dazzled in the Texas sun, while Haas’s American flag-themed VF-24, complete with stars and an eagle, celebrated local pride. Alpine’s orange livery promoted Indiana Jones and the Great Circle, adding flair. Off-track, over 100,000 fans enjoyed concerts by Eminem and Sting, with the F1 Fanzone buzzing with SimRacing and Pit Stop Challenges. COTA’s vibrant atmosphere made it a weekend to remember.

COTA 2024

The 2024 United States Grand Prix at COTA delivered everything F1 fans crave: Ferrari’s dominant 1-2, Verstappen and Norris’s fierce duel, Lawson’s rookie heroics, and a vibrant fan experience. From Turn 1 chaos to podium drama, Austin proved why it’s a fan-favorite track. Dive into the drivers and teams that made this race epic, and get ready for more thrills in 2025!
