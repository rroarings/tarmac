Liam Lawson, born on February 11, 2002, in Hastings, New Zealand, faces a season of recalibration in 2025 after a brief stint at Red Bull Racing was cut short post-Japanese Grand Prix, with Yuki Tsunoda taking his place. Now back with Racing Bulls, Lawson returns to familiar territory—but with more experience and even greater determination to prove he deserves another shot at the main team. Paired with rookie Isack Hadjar, Lawson is expected to lead the team both on and off the track.

Lawson’s time in Formula 1 so far has been marked by flashes of serious talent: confident overtakes, intelligent tire management, and strong adaptability across circuits. While the mid-season swap was a setback, it hasn’t shaken his focus. Back at Racing Bulls, he’s been tasked with anchoring the team’s development while continuing to rack up points and outperform expectations.

Still only in his early 20s, Lawson’s journey in F1 is far from over. With the Red Bull driver pipeline as fluid as ever, consistent performances and strong results at Racing Bulls could easily open the door to another promotion. For now, Lawson is focused on maximizing his 2025 campaign—fuelled by a renewed hunger and a point to prove.
