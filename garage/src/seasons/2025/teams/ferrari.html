Ferrari enters the 2025 Formula 1 season with high expectations, fielding the SF-25 driven by Charles Leclerc and seven-time champion Lewis Hamilton, who joined from Mercedes in a blockbuster move. The SF-25, unveiled with a red livery and prominent HP branding, features a new pull-rod front suspension to boost performance, though it trails McLaren slightly in pace. Hamilton secured sprint pole and victory in China, while Leclerc’s podium in Saudi Arabia marked Ferrari’s first of the season, positioning them second in the Constructors’ standings. Despite Hamilton struggling with the car’s handling in Jeddah, Ferrari’s upgrades and driver talent keep them in contention for their first championship since 2008.

Under Team Principal Fred Vasseur, Ferrari focuses on closing the performance gap to McLaren while managing the dynamic between Leclerc and Hamilton, both vying for the Drivers’ Championship. The SF-25’s upgrades, tested extensively at Fiorano, address 2024’s tyre wear issues, but Hamilton’s struggles with the car’s balance in Jeddah highlight areas for refinement. Ferrari’s strong commercial backing and fanbase fuel their ambition to end a 17-year title drought, with the 24-race calendar offering ample opportunities to capitalize on their improved reliability.

Ferrari faces challenges in maintaining harmony between Leclerc, eager to cement his leadership, and Hamilton, chasing an eighth title. The SF-25’s sensitivity to windy conditions, evident in Bahrain, requires ongoing development to match McLaren’s tyre management. With 2026 regulation changes looming, Ferrari balances immediate competitiveness with long-term planning, aiming to sustain their early-season momentum through critical triple-headers in Japan, Bahrain, and Saudi Arabia to secure both championships.
